Icelandic Water

Posted by Drew Hartsfield on December 13, 2021

As I was trying to figure out cache sizes for my computer systems class and listening to Nightwalk by Parcels, I was reminded of a morning in Iceland. After visiting Elephant Rock, we drove back to the town of Vestmannaeyjabær island. We had some time to spare before nestling our white land cruiser in the belly of a large Icelandic ferry, so we decided to grab some pastries at a local shop, probably the only pastry shop on the small island. As we chose our respective pastries, our conversation with the shop lady turned to that of water -an often- frequented conversation in Iceland. She outlined that all businesses in Iceland were required to refill water bottles if requested. She described with intent and passion that no plastic water, even if Icelandic, could compete with water from the tap. As she refilled our water bottles, and I once again drank from my chipped and dirtied white hydro flask, I tasted something I hadn’t before. I’m unsure if I actually tasted a different flavor, or tasted anything at all, but I know I did feel something different while drinking.

After many smiles, and contented stomachs, we left the pastry shop and resumed our land cruiser seat positions. I can’t remember if I was driving or not -I think I was- but I do remember looking at the large rock at Vestmannaeyjabær’s port. I reminisced of my first sight at Morro Bay the summer before. Everything felt so grandiose. As we spent our final moments driving on Vestmannaeyjabær land, I once again turned my thoughts to that of water. How special that the shop lady was so passionate about water and sustainability. How fortunate were we to be educated of a law and attitude that works so well in lands far from home? Passion is a trait I value so highly. It really does enact change and make you feel things you haven’t before. Don’t die without passion... I sure won’t.
